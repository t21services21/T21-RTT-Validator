# Pathway 1 – Unit 7 Quiz
## Foundations Capstone Project

Total questions: **10**  
Suggested pass mark: **8 / 10 (80%)**

This quiz is mainly reflective and should be completed **after** the
capstone project.

---

## Section A – Multiple Choice (5 questions)

**Q1.** Which of the following is **not** usually part of a good data
project write-up?

A. Clear problem statement and objectives.  
B. Description of data and methods used.  
C. Main findings and recommendations.  
D. Long blocks of code pasted with no explanation.

**Correct answer:** D

---

**Q2.** Why is it important to describe **limitations** of your project?

A. To show that the project is not useful.  
B. To be honest about what the analysis can and cannot support, and to build
   trust.  
C. To make the report longer.  
D. It is not important; limitations should be hidden.

**Correct answer:** B

---

**Q3.** Which of the following is the **best** description of a capstone
project for your portfolio?

A. A random collection of unrelated charts.  
B. An end-to-end analysis on a realistic dataset with clear narrative and
   code.  
C. A single SQL query.  
D. A screenshot of your IDE.

**Correct answer:** B

---

**Q4.** Why is version control (e.g. Git/GitHub) useful for your capstone
project?

A. It automatically improves performance of your code.  
B. It allows you to track changes, back up work and share projects easily
   with others.  
C. It is only needed for large teams.  
D. It replaces the need for documentation.

**Correct answer:** B

---

**Q5.** Which of the following is the **best** way to present your capstone
project to employers?

A. Send them your raw dataset without explanation.  
B. Share a GitHub link with a clear README and selected notebooks/reports.  
C. Email them every notebook you created.  
D. Share only a CV with no project links.

**Correct answer:** B

---

## Section B – Short Answer (5 questions)

**Q6.** In 3–4 sentences, summarise the **business problem** your capstone
project addressed.

**Model answer (guide):**  
Learner should describe the context, who cares about the problem, and what
they were trying to improve or understand.

---

**Q7.** List at least three **skills** you used in your capstone project
(e.g. Python, Pandas, SQL, statistics, visualisation) and give one sentence
on how each was applied.

**Model answer (guide):**  
For example: used Python to load and clean data, Pandas to group and
aggregate, SQL to pull data from a database, and visualisation libraries to
create charts for the report.

---

**Q8.** Describe one challenge you faced during the capstone and how you
solved or worked around it.

**Model answer (guide):**  
Learner should mention a realistic issue (e.g. messy data, unclear question,
errors in code) and a practical solution.

---

**Q9.** If you had **more time or more data**, what would you add or change
in your project?

**Model answer (guide):**  
For example, bringing in additional datasets, testing more models, or
improving visual design and documentation.

---

**Q10.** In 3–5 sentences, explain how your capstone project shows that you
are ready for an entry-level data role in your preferred country and
sector.

**Model answer (guide):**  
Learner should connect the project to real job tasks (cleaning data,
answering business questions, communicating results) and highlight
transferable skills.
